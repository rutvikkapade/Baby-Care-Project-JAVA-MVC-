ROBO Canoeing

Problem statement :

Design a Wireless controlled Robotic-boat/ aquabot/ robo canoeing capable of floating on water and complete specified task within provided time. The rounds will be decided according to groups participated. The Robotic boat which gains maximum points will be the winner.


Rules

This is a racing event. Two robotic boats A and B have to start simultaneously from their starting points. So, fastest and most balanced robot will win. No offensive strategies are allowed. It might lead to disqualification. Each team can have maximum four members. Students from different institutes can form a team. Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification. Event-coordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event. Boats can be build from toolkits. But, readymade boats are not allowed. Judge's decision will be considered final.


Robo Specifications


The maximum dimension of the Robotic boat can be 30 x 25 cm x 15cm (l x b x h). Maximum weight should not exceed 3kg The power supply should not be more than 15 volt.


Arena Specification


Arena is rectangular pool and consists of water up to a height of 30 cm. The arena is divided into two parts so as to allow two teams to compete against each other simultaneously. The participants are supposed to bring their own Robotic boats for the competition The track will be curved path disclosed just before the competition. There will be certain obstacles in the pool which will try to slow down the boat.


Penalties


if robo will go out of track line it will leads to penalty. If robo touches the hurdles it leads to penalty. 3 touches are allowed (humen touches) after that penalty will be given may cause to disqualification


Certification policies


Top two teams will be awarded by certification of excellence and cash prize for the event. All teams will be provided by certificate of participation. The winner has to complete the task, if the fail to complete the task then they will be not eligible for certificate.


all rules are subject to change**
