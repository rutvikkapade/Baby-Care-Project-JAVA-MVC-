ROBO RACE (HURDLES)

Problem statement :

The team has to design a manually operated wired robot capable to overcome obstacles or hurdles in the path within the shortest period of time.


Rules

The team should not consist of more than 4 members. Each member from same college is mandatory. The robot should follow the robot specifications provided. Any deviation from the mentioned specifications will lead to disqualification. No test practice will be allowed on the main arena. AC supply will be provided in the college. The arena may subject to change before the commencement of any round. The structure of the robot should not be changed during the competition. If the Robot crosses a checkpoint, and moves off track, then the Robot would be placed back on the previous checkpoint crossed with penalty. If the robot by any chances falls from the bridge, the robot will be kept back to the previous checkpoint crossed with a penalty. Once the race begins, three hand touches are allowed, if you are taking hand touch you have to start from last check point. During the game play, if any part of a robot is destructed/ dismantled/ damaged the participant will be given a timeout to repair at an expense of a penalty, while the next participant for will be called the play. Only 3 timeout will be given for any participant and the participant will repair it without the help from the mentor. The robot that will complete the specified task in least time will be the winner. Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification. Faculty-coordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event. The decision of the judges will be final and abiding. Argument with judges in any form will lead to the disqualification of the team.


Robo Specifications


The maximum dimension of the robot can be 30 x 30 x 30cm. Wired or wireless robots are allowed. The length of the wire should be long enough to cover the whole track and wire should remain slack during the complete run. Readymade toys car or lego kits are strictly not allowed. Net weight should not exceed 3 kg. However a tolerance of 5 % is acceptable. The maximum allowed voltage to power the robot is 12v.


Arena Specification


The track surface and course line may have unevenness. There might be abrupt angles, but these will not exceed 30 degrees. There will be certain obstacles in the race track which will try to slow down the robot. Arena will consist of switch gate, speed breakers, marble pit, slippery path, rotating disc, curve ramp Down, seesaw, balloon pit, slotted ramp etc.

Round 1


Round 1 consist of all the hurdles and participants has to complete the round 1 in minimum time.


Round 2


Difficulty level of hurdles will be increased.


Penalties


The participant must not touch the bot during the run without taking a timeout doing so will result in a penalty. If the bot touches the track line points will be deducted for each intersection. If the bot is fully out of the track double points will be deducted as of intersection. If the participant wishes to skip an obstacle, a specific amount of point will be deducted depending upon the difficulty of the obstacle. After all three timeouts touch is not allowed, if do so leads to disqualification.


Certification policies


Top two teams will be awarded by certification of excellence and cash prize for the event. All teams will be provided by certificate of participation. The winner has to complete the task, if the fail to complete the task then they will be not eligible for certificate.


all rules are subject to change**
